\section{Conclusion}

Agriculture in India is more than just a means of livelihood—it is a way of life for millions and a cornerstone of the nation’s socio-economic fabric. While the sector has shown resilience over the decades, it now faces the dual challenge of ensuring food security and improving farmers’ income amid environmental, technological, and market transitions. The path forward lies in transforming agriculture from a survival activity to a sustainable and profitable enterprise through inclusive reforms, innovation, and investment. A future-ready Indian agriculture must be climate-smart, market-linked, and farmer-centric—only then can it truly become the engine of rural prosperity and national development.
